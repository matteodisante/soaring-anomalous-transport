\section{The IGC tracklog format}
\label{impl:igcformat}
The parser (\path{soaring.analysis.igc.parse_igc}) checks, at decode time and
independently of any flight-dynamics judgement: that the time is a valid 24-hour
\texttt{hh:mm:ss}; that the arc-minutes field of latitude and longitude is
\texttt{<\,60} (the \path{DDMM.mmm} encoding is undefined otherwise); that the
hemisphere letter is one of the two valid characters; and that the decoded
latitude/longitude fall within $[-90,90]$/$[-180,180]$ degrees. A record failing any of
these is dropped as unparseable, on the same footing as a record with the wrong length or
a non-numeric field. This format-validity check is implemented now and covered by unit
tests; the fix-level dynamics-plausibility checks it is distinct from
(Sec.~\ref{impl:fixlevel}) are designed and thresholded but not yet implemented.

\subsection{Choice of altitude channel}
\label{impl:altchannel}

\paragraph{PSD sample (Figure~\ref{fig:altnoise}a--c).} A seeded random subsample of
\num{400} flights per discipline. Panel~(a)/(b) use one representative flight (the one
with the most fixes in the subsample); its measured offset (panel~b) is annotated on the
panel itself, not restated here.

\paragraph{Welch/PSD computational procedure (panel c).} IGC loggers record one fix per
second nominally, but timestamps are not guaranteed to be perfectly regular (a missed
fix, a logger writing at \SI{1.05}{\second} instead of \SI{1.00}{\second}); Welch's
method (Appendix~\ref{app:psd}) requires a uniformly sampled signal, so the following
runs on every flight before the transform.
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item \textbf{Modal sampling period.} For each flight the median inter-fix interval is
        computed and rounded to the nearest integer second. The value that appears most
        often across all flights in the subsample -- the \emph{mode} -- is taken as the
        common target $\Delta t$ (in practice $\Delta t=\SI{1}{\second}$ for both
        disciplines, giving $f_s=\SI{1}{\hertz}$ and $f_{\text{Nyq}}=\SI{0.5}{\hertz}$).
  \item \textbf{Flight selection.} Only flights whose own median period lies within
        \SI{0.25}{\second} of the modal $\Delta t$ are retained, so pooled spectra share
        one frequency axis; flights with fewer than $N_{\text{seg}}=256$ fixes
        ($\sim$\SI{4}{\minute} at $\Delta t=\SI{1}{\second}$) are also excluded, since at
        least one full segment is needed. Of the \num{400} sampled flights per
        discipline, this leaves \num{212} paragliders but only \num{114} hang gliders:
        paragliders are overwhelmingly \SI{1}{\second} loggers, so the population mode is
        a good match for most of them, whereas hang gliders split across several native
        rates ($1$, $2$, $5$, $\geq\!8\,\text{s}$, Figure~\ref{fig:sampling}) and only
        their \SI{1}{\second}-logger minority survives this cut.
  \item \textbf{Uniform resampling.} The altitude series $z(t)$ is linearly interpolated
        onto a grid $t_0,t_0+\Delta t,t_0+2\Delta t,\ldots$ spanning the flight, without
        introducing frequency content beyond $f_{\text{Nyq}}$.
  \item \textbf{Welch on the resampled series.} \path{scipy.signal.welch} is called
        with $f_s=1/\Delta t$, $N_{\text{seg}}=256$, 50\% overlap, a Hann window, and
        linear detrending per segment.
  \item \textbf{Ensemble average.} The PSD of each qualifying flight is added to a
        running sum, separately per discipline and channel (barometric/GNSS); the mean
        over all contributing flights is the curve shown in Figure~\ref{fig:altnoise}(c).
        Because $\Delta t$ is the same for every retained flight by construction, the
        figure's Nyquist dotted line is a single fixed value, not a flight-by-flight
        average.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Fallback-rate census (panel d).} A full census, not a sample: the same full
parse of every track is needed anyway for the flight-level filtering diagnostics of
Sec.~\ref{impl:flightfilter} (Figure~\ref{fig:preproc}), so the barometric-presence
fraction is read off that one scan rather than measured separately -- exactness is a
byproduct, not extra work. (A sized random sample -- the standard proportion formula
$n=z^2p(1-p)/e^2$ -- would be the right tool if a full census were not already being
paid for elsewhere; it is not needed here.)

\subsection{Fix-level cleaning}
\label{impl:fixlevel}

\paragraph{Status.} Format-validity rejection is implemented in
\path{soaring.analysis.igc.parse_igc} and covered by unit tests. Dynamics-plausibility
rejection is designed and thresholded (Table~\ref{tab:cleaning}), but the routine that
walks a trajectory applying the bounds fix by fix does not yet exist; building it is the
next step at this stage.

\paragraph{Configuration.} All six bounds of Table~\ref{tab:cleaning} are the
\texttt{FixLevelThresholds} dataclass, loaded by
\path{soaring.analysis.preprocessing.load_preproc_config} from
\path{configs/preprocessing.yaml} (key \path{fix_level}); nothing is hard-coded in
Python.

\paragraph{Diagnostic sample (Figure~\ref{fig:fixlevel}).} Computed by
\path{fix_level_distributions} and rendered by
\path{make_fixlevel_diagnostics_figure} (Table~\ref{tab:impl-repro}). Panels~(a)--(c)
pool a seeded random sample of \num{15000} paraglider flights (\num{126723499}
consecutive-fix pairs, $\sim$\SI{73}{\second} to compute) and the complete
\num{6716}-flight hang-glider population (a full census at this size, \num{37915334}
pairs, $\sim$\SI{25}{\second}) -- under two minutes total. The large sample is
deliberate: resolving the sparse artefact tail well enough to distinguish it from real
dynamics needs far more points than a headline statistic would.

\paragraph{Bound-placement procedure.} The horizontal-speed bound is placed at the point
where the bin-to-bin ratio of a finely binned (\SI{2.5}{\metre\per\second}) histogram
settles at $\approx 1$ (decay has stopped), not at the point where the decay first
visibly slows ($\approx\SI{40}{\metre\per\second}$ for hang gliders, versus the adopted
\SI{55}{\metre\per\second}).

\paragraph{Axis choices.} The $x$-range in (a)/(b) extends to each discipline's own
99.99th percentile rather than cropping just past the bound; panel~(c) keeps a narrower
range since its cut sits far out in an otherwise tight, unimodal distribution.

\paragraph{Integer-quantization check (panel b).} Not every flight samples at
\SI{1}{\second} (Figure~\ref{fig:sampling}), and differencing over a longer native step
returns a finer-grained set of values (e.g.\ multiples of \SI{0.2}{\metre\per\second} at
a \SI{5}{\second} cadence) that partially fill the gaps once every cadence is pooled.
Checked directly: among fixes from \SI{1}{\second}-native flights, about 99\% land
within \SI{0.05}{\metre\per\second} of an integer; among fixes from every other native
cadence, only 34--42\% do.

\paragraph{Reproduce.} Table~\ref{tab:impl-repro}, row \path{generate_preproc_figure.py}
(one run also regenerates Figures~\ref{fig:preproc}, \ref{fig:gaps}, \ref{fig:sampling}
from a shared full-census scan; the fix-level panels use a separate sampled pass).

\subsection{Trimming of ground phases}
\label{impl:trimming}
The take-off speed (\SI{5}{\metre\per\second}) and sustained duration (\SI{30}{\second})
are the \texttt{TrimmingThresholds} dataclass, loaded from
\path{configs/preprocessing.yaml} (key \texttt{trimming}); as with every threshold in
this pipeline, nothing is hard-coded in Python.

\subsection{Flight-level filtering}
\label{impl:flightfilter}
The duration ($T\geq\SI{40}{\minute}$) and path-length ($L\geq\SI{30}{\kilo\metre}$) cuts
are the \texttt{FlightLevelThresholds} dataclass, from \path{configs/preprocessing.yaml}
(key \path{flight_level}).

\paragraph{Diagnostic sample (Figure~\ref{fig:preproc}).} Computed by
\path{load_or_scan_tracks} (via \path{scan_tracks}/\path{track_stats}) and
rendered by \path{make_flightlevel_diagnostics_figure}, on the full census -- every
parsed track, no sub-sampling: \num{186025} paraglider and \num{6716} hang-glider tracks.
Median duration and path length are \SI{2.7}{\hour}/\SI{86}{\kilo\metre} for paragliders
and \SI{3.1}{\hour}/\SI{158}{\kilo\metre} for hang gliders -- the latter flying faster and
farther. This same scan (cached at \path{<data_root>/derived/track_scan.parquet}) also
feeds Figures~\ref{fig:gaps} and~\ref{fig:sampling}, so all three share one full-census
pass.

\paragraph{Reproduce.} Table~\ref{tab:impl-repro}, row \path{generate_preproc_figure.py}.

\subsection{Uniform sampling within a flight}
\label{impl:uniform}
The gap bound ($10\times$ native $\Delta t$) and maximum missing fraction ($0.10$) are the
\texttt{SamplingThresholds} dataclass, from \path{configs/preprocessing.yaml} (key
\texttt{sampling}).

\paragraph{Figures~\ref{fig:gaps} and~\ref{fig:sampling}.} Both rendered by
\path{make_gap_diagnostics_figure}/\path{make_sampling_figure} from the same
full-census scan as Figure~\ref{fig:preproc} (Table~\ref{tab:impl-repro}, row
\path{generate_preproc_figure.py}) -- no separate parse needed.

\paragraph{Gap-ratio discreteness (Figure~\ref{fig:gaps}).} Checked directly: only
86\%/63\% (para/hang) of largest-gap-ratio values are exact integers, the rest a
genuinely continuous scatter; the integer peaks visible for hang gliders sit around
$k=3,6,10,15$.

\paragraph{Native-rate discreteness (Figure~\ref{fig:sampling}).} Checked directly:
$\Delta t$ lands on an exact whole second for 99.9\% of paragliders and 100\% of hang
gliders. Per-rate breakdown: paragliders overwhelmingly (69\%) at \SI{1}{\second}; hang
gliders spread across \SI{1}{\second} (30\%), \SI{5}{\second} (27\%), \SI{10}{\second}
(15\%), \SI{2}{\second} (13\%), and a long thin tail beyond \SI{11}{\second}.
